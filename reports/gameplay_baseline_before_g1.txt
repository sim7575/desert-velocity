1. Main scene
project.godot run/main_scene is res://scenes/main/Boot.tscn. Boot is a Node named DesertVelocity whose only attached production script is res://scripts/game_manager.gd.

2. Boot -> game flow
Boot instantiates GameManager. _ready() creates missing runtime input actions, loads SaveManager data, creates AudioManager and opens the main menu. PROVA SPECIALE or ENDURANCE selects run_mode and opens the garage. CONFERMA E PARTI calls start_game(), which saves current settings, clears the previous scene, builds the Environment and sun, creates VehicleController, RoadManager, CameraController and GameHUD, connects gameplay signals, resets run state and starts either immediately (ENDURANCE) or after a four-second countdown (STAGE).

3. Race objective
ENDURANCE: continue until fuel or integrity reaches zero, collecting bonuses and attempting to beat the saved score record. STAGE: traverse the effective 64x52 m route (about 3,328 m), pass six ordered geometric checkpoints and reach route index 63; official time is driving time plus penalties. The former 3,200 m wording was obsolete documentation, not gameplay geometry.

4. Controls
W/Up accelerate; S/Down brake then reverse; A/D or Left/Right steer; Space handbrake; C cycles four camera views; Esc pauses/resumes; R returns to a safe route transform; F3 toggles telemetry; F4 cycles atmosphere. Enter confirms focused menu buttons. While paused, R restarts and M returns to menu.

5. Acceleration
Throttle is Input.get_axis("brake", "accelerate") and is smoothed at 2.3 units/s for the 1420 kg Stallion or 3.2 units/s for the lighter GT. Base acceleration comes from VehicleData, then surface acceleration, damage_level and turbo multipliers are applied. Off-road and soft-boundary multipliers further reduce acceleration. Existing runtime tests prove both vehicles accelerate above 15 m/s, but do not freeze exact acceleration curves or times.

6. Braking
Negative throttle while moving forward approaches zero using vehicle brake multiplied by surface brake. Space applies an additional approach-to-zero rate of 18 m/s² and reduces target lateral grip through handbrake_blend. Exact stopping distances are not protected by an automated assertion.

7. Steering
Steering uses speed-dependent authority and a speed-reduced steer limit, modified by air state, damage and off-road state. Road alignment assistance applies only on-road with near-zero steering input above 2 m/s. Lateral velocity is grip-damped and clamped by max_slip. Runtime tests protect slip cap and physical off-road recovery, not exact steering response across the full speed range.

8. Reverse
When brake remains pressed below 1 m/s, speed approaches -28% of the current maximum using 65% of acceleration. Direction sign reverses steering. Runtime tests explicitly verify reverse speed below -2 m/s for both vehicles.

9. Gearbox and gears
There is no drivetrain or player-controlled shifting. simulated_gear is a six-step display value derived from speed/max_speed: clamp(int(ratio*5)+1, 1, 6). No existing test protects this mapping.

10. Turbo/boost
Turbo is granted by collectible kind 3 and activate_turbo() sets turbo_time to 5.0 seconds. While active, maximum speed is multiplied by 1.35 and acceleration by 1.22. Camera target FOV changes from 70 to 79 and the HUD bar displays turbo_time*20. No test protects duration, multipliers, consumption/countdown, HUD mapping or activation through the collectible signal.

11. Fuel
Fuel starts at 100. Drain per frame is 0.72*delta*(0.3+abs(speed)/40). Fuel collectible kind 0 adds 25 up to 100. Fuel <= 0 triggers generic game over in both modes. No automated test protects start value, drain formula, pickup or loss condition.

12. Integrity
Integrity starts at 100. Obstacle collisions emit metadata damage (10+2*kind); sustained off-road use starts damaging after 3 seconds at 3 integrity/s; reset removes 8 but clamps at 1. Landing impacts change VehicleController.damage_level rather than GameManager health. Health <= 0 triggers generic game over. No automated test protects these values or their HUD/result effects.

13. Score
Driving attempts to add int(abs(speed)*delta*multiplier*0.8) every frame; score collectible kind 1 adds 500*multiplier; reset removes 250 down to zero. This is ambiguous/contradictory: converting each frame contribution to int makes driving score frame-rate dependent and normally zero at 60 FPS for non-turbo maximum speed. No automated test protects scoring.

14. Record
Only show_game_over() compares ENDURANCE/generic score with save.record and persists a new record. Stage completion has no saved best-time record. No automated test protects record update, non-update, display or persistence.

15. Multiplier
Collectible kind 4 sets multiplier=2 for 10 seconds; expiry restores 1. It affects score additions. No automated test protects activation, duration, expiry or score interaction.

16. Checkpoints
STAGE uses ordered target route indices [9,19,29,39,49,59]. A checkpoint advances only when the nearest active segment has reached the current target and the player is within the logical road width/segment extent. runtime_verification protects countdown and one geometric checkpoint advance; it does not drive through or independently validate all six.

17. Finish
STAGE finishes when checkpoint count is at least six and nearest route index is at least 63. runtime_verification directly protects transition to the results screen after these conditions are arranged.

18. Timer
stage_time advances only after countdown while GAME is active and unpaused. Official result is stage_time+stage_penalty. No automated test protects timer rate, pause exclusion or result formatting.

19. Penalties
Accelerating during countdown adds 0.5 seconds per real second; off-road beyond three seconds adds 0.35 seconds per real second; collision damage >=14 adds 2 seconds; any manual/automatic reposition in STAGE adds 5 seconds. No automated test protects these calculations.

20. Off-road behavior
Road state is logical distance from the nearest segment center. Outside 8.5 m is off-road; beyond 22 m is DEEP_SAND/soft boundary; at 42 m the vehicle is reset. Off-road reduces max speed and acceleration, changes grip, adds recovery traction and eventually damages health. Runtime tests protect deep-sand detection, re-entry, retained lateral speed and hard-limit reset.

21. Surfaces
ASPHALT, GRAVEL, SAND and DEEP_SAND provide independent longitudinal grip, lateral grip, acceleration, brake, drag and roughness data. Stage segments preserve authored ASPHALT/GRAVEL metadata; off-road maps to SAND/DEEP_SAND. runtime_verification protects asphalt+gravel presence and deep-sand detection, but not every coefficient or their exact vehicle effect.

22. Collisions
VehicleController remains CharacterBody3D with a 2.5x1.25x4.7 m BoxShape3D, layer 2, mask 1|4. Road uses a continuous top-only ConcavePolygonShape3D; jump segments replace the flat surface with matched profile mesh/collision. Obstacles are StaticBody3D members of group obstacle. Stallion V2/GT integration tests protect simplified collision dimensions; road_seam_verification protects 144 seam crossings.

23. Obstacles
ENDURANCE randomly spawns one of six obstacle types; STAGE deterministically adds shoulder obstacles on sufficiently sharp route segments. Collision cuts speed to 38%, applies metadata damage and grants 1.1 seconds of collision invulnerability. Existing tests prove spawn transformation follows curves and seam crossings after test obstacle removal; obstacle damage/penalty logic is not protected.

24. Victory conditions
STAGE victory/result requires six checkpoints plus route index >=63. ENDURANCE has no victory condition. Stage finish transition is protected by runtime_verification.

25. Defeat conditions
Fuel <=0 or health <=0 calls generic show_game_over(). There is no other explicit defeat state. These conditions are not protected by automated tests.

26. Saves
user://desert_velocity.cfg stores record, vehicle, legacy volume, music volume, SFX volume, mute and graphics quality through ConfigFile. It does not store active race state, stage time, checkpoint progress or vehicle unlocks. No existing test protects defaults, round-trip persistence, schema compatibility or corrupt-file handling.

27. Progression
Current progression is limited to saved vehicle selection and score record; both vehicles are always selectable and no unlock/campaign progression exists. The requested invariant named "progression" is therefore not backed by a distinct implemented progression system or test.

29. Ambiguous, contradictory or unprotected rules — STOP CONDITIONS

Resolved and characterized findings
- Score: the original int(abs(speed)*delta*multiplier*0.8) discarded every sub-point frame contribution. G1-B preserves the theoretical |speed|*multiplier*0.8 points/second rate through a fractional carry reset on every new race. Deterministic 11-second simulation at 17.3 m/s: 30 FPS=152, 60 FPS=152, 120 FPS=152, theoretical=152.24.
- Route length: geometry, checkpoints and finish remain unchanged at 64 segments x 52 m, approximately 3,328 m. The 3,200 m documentation was corrected; unused production constants were not repurposed.
