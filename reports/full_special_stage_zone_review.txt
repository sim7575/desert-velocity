Scope
- Visual composition only. Route, stage_layout, gameplay, physics, collisions, camera, Stallion V3, HUD, effects, logical checkpoints, saves and progression are unchanged.
- Exact zone boundaries remain 0-9, 10-17, 18-28, 29-39, 40-47, 48-56 and 57-63.
- Existing Environment Kit V2 meshes and approved G1-D.1 materials remain the source. Local zone materials are runtime duplicates with palette parameters only; source materials are unchanged.

Zone 1 | Desert Start / Open Flats | 0-9
- Dedicated collision-free PARTENZA gate replaces only the red endpoint visual.
- Two light paddock shelters, lateral markers and a distant rocky anticipation cue establish the race start while preserving the open central axis.
- Identity: light ochre, low controlled density and long sightline.

Zone 2 | Eroded Rock Corridor | 10-17
- Progressive left-dominant wall and broken right wall form an open corridor without roof or route intrusion.
- Secondary rocks, talus/contact scatter and sparse vegetation ground the wall bases.
- Identity: burnt earth and brown, increasing perceived enclosure with deliberate openings.

Zone 3 | Canyon Approach | 18-28
- Distant mesa announces the zone; asymmetric canyon fins then increase height and local contrast toward the ridge.
- CP2 and the CP3 approach remain unobstructed; the sky remains continuously visible.
- Identity: muted red rock with cooler shadow palette and layered depth.

Zone 4 | High Plateau | 29-39
- Distant and mid-distance mesas describe plateau edges without adding physical precipices.
- Low broad rocks and a crest sign preserve the largest intentional negative space and horizon readability.
- Identity: desaturated warm gray/beige panorama rather than an empty terrain plane.

Zone 5 | Dunes and Wreck | 40-47
- The approved survey rover is enlarged and grouped with a hazard sign, abstract debris and a partially overlapping dune outside the road corridor.
- A secondary dune composition carries the transition toward gravel without turning the area into a scrapyard.
- Identity: golden sand and subdued oxidized metal narrative cluster.

Zone 6 | Technical Gravel Pass | 48-56
- Unequal-height walls follow the gentle curve and create two open visual gates.
- Direction signs, a safety barrier, edge debris and base scatter signal technical rhythm before steering input.
- CP5, the road, jumps and sightlines remain clear.
- Identity: darker brown/cool rock, closer safe lateral masses and asymmetric rhythm.

Zone 7 | Final Golden Run | 57-63
- Distant warm mesas begin the crescendo; two large asymmetric canyon fins at segment 61 create an open natural gate.
- Lateral markers lead to a dedicated collision-free TRAGUARDO gate with warm-white and ochre accents.
- The center remains clear and the finish is visible in advance.
- Identity: warm ochre culmination and the strongest visual axis of the stage.

RockArch decision
- RockArch_A remains deferred. Its candidate area is close to CP5 and a curved section; forcing it would risk camera clearance and checkpoint readability.
- The approved alternative is an open natural gate without a roof, built from asymmetric canyon fins. It introduces no collider and requires no route change.

Density, LOD and visibility
- Full composition: LOD0 4, LOD1 70, LOD2 646.
- 28 MultiMesh groups aggregate 690 instances, down from the G1-F baseline of 710.
- Small scatter was reduced and redistributed toward zones 2, 3, 5, 6 and 7; 64 contact instances ground key formations.
- Fixed authored LOD and distance visibility retain margins of at least 18 m. A 320-frame moving pass found no grave popping or persistent transition slowdown.

HUD 0 m investigation
- With the normal two-segment look-ahead present, 0 m occurred once in 320 controlled motion samples: at the exact center of the final clamped pacenote target.
- Maximum duration was one sample. It did not persist and is not a HUD state bug.
- Repeated 0 m values in some automatic screenshots were caused by freezing/recycling the fixture at an exact segment center without its normal look-ahead. HUD logic was not modified.
