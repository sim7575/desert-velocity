1. Garage

Cause:
- The old camera called look_at() before entering the SceneTree. Godot 4.7 rejected the call, so the camera could retain an invalid/default orientation.
- The full-viewport preview had no dedicated screen region and was composed behind a 445 px overlay panel and its buttons.

Repair:
- Added a named, controlled GaragePreviewCamera, inserted into the SceneTree before look_at().
- Reorganized the left panel to 390 px (30.5% of 1280 px) and declared an 818 px right preview safe area.
- Camera framing is independent from the approved gameplay chase camera.
- Measured visible vehicle bounds: Stallion x=654.83..953.36, y=262.57..455.48; Bavarian x=628.60..961.40, y=286.89..469.78.
- All statistics, vehicle models/materials and keyboard/mouse navigation remain unchanged.

2. Vehicle/scenario parity

Cause:
- VehicleFactory did not select a scenario; it only selected the vehicle visual/statistics. The normal Stage launch, however, relied on mutable global fallback flags and did not bind a canonical scenario path at the GameManager boundary.

Repair:
- RoadManager now accepts an instance-level stage_visual_profile_path.
- GameManager explicitly requests FullSpecialStageVisualExpansion.tscn for the normal Stage path, independently of save.vehicle.
- Disabled global flags still expose the old original and Environment V2 pilot as explicit technical fallbacks; they are never selected from the vehicle index.

Runtime proof:
- Desert Stallion 65 scenario_id=G1-F.1_FULL_STAGE.
- Bavarian GT-R scenario_id=G1-F.1_FULL_STAGE.
- Both vehicles traversed checkpoints [1,2,3,4,5,6] and reached GAME_OVER/finish.
- Pause, resume, Stage restart, return to menu and persisted Bavarian selection passed.
- Original scenario fallback, Environment V2 pilot fallback and Stallion V2 fallback passed when explicitly requested.
